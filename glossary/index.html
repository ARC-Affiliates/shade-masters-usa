Window Treatments Glossary

Definitions of Window Treatments terms as used by Shade Masters USA in Leesburg, FL. 22 terms defined.

Jump to: B · C · F · H · I · L · M · O · P · R · S · T · V

B

Blackout Shades

Blackout shades use opaque materials and tight installation methods to eliminate nearly all incoming light. They are ideal for bedrooms, home theaters, and shift workers, and they also provide an added layer of insulation and sound dampening.

C

Cellular Shades

Also called honeycomb shades, these window coverings have a pleated fabric structure with hollow cells that trap air. The trapped air acts as insulation, reducing heat transfer through the window and lowering energy bills in hot or cold climates.

Cordless Lift

Cordless lift systems eliminate exposed pull cords, allowing the user to raise or lower the shade by gently pushing or pulling the bottom rail. This design is a child- and pet-safe feature now required by national window covering safety standards.

Custom Window Treatments

Custom window treatments are made to the exact measurements and specifications of each individual window, rather than purchased in standard sizes. They ensure a precise fit, broader fabric and feature selection, and a polished look that off-the-shelf products cannot match.

F

Faux Wood Blinds

Faux wood blinds are made from composite materials or PVC designed to mimic the look of real wood. They resist warping, fading, and moisture, making them well-suited for humid Florida climates, kitchens, and bathrooms where real wood would deteriorate.

H

Headrail

The headrail is the horizontal housing at the top of a blind or shade that holds the operating mechanism, lift cords, or motor. Some treatments offer a decorative valance to conceal the headrail for a finished, custom appearance.

Hurricane Shades

Hurricane shades, sometimes called storm shades, are exterior roll-down coverings designed to protect windows from wind-driven debris during tropical storms. They provide impact resistance, added insulation, and security, and may qualify homeowners for insurance discounts in coastal regions.

I

In-Home Consultation

An in-home consultation is a no-obligation appointment where a window treatment specialist visits the property to assess windows, show fabric and material samples, and provide accurate measurements. It helps homeowners visualize options in their actual lighting and décor before purchasing.

Inside Mount

An inside mount installation places the window treatment inside the window frame, creating a clean, built-in appearance that showcases the trim. This mount requires precise depth measurements to ensure the brackets and headrail fit properly within the casing.

L

Light Filtering

Light filtering refers to fabrics or materials that allow soft, diffused daylight to pass through while still providing daytime privacy. These treatments brighten a room without harsh glare but do not fully block outside views at night when interior lights are on.

Light Gap

The light gap is the small space around the edges of a window covering where light leaks through. Inside-mount installations and side channels are used to minimize light gaps, especially important for bedrooms and media rooms requiring blackout conditions.

M

Motorized Shades

Motorized shades use a small internal motor to raise, lower, or tilt the covering with a remote, wall switch, or app. They are ideal for hard-to-reach windows, large openings, and homeowners who want to eliminate dangling cords for safety.

O

Openness Factor

Openness factor is the percentage of weave in a solar shade fabric that allows light and view-through. A lower percentage like 3% blocks more glare and heat while offering more privacy, while a higher percentage preserves a clearer outside view.

Outside Mount

An outside mount installs the window covering on the wall or trim above the window opening. This option is used when the window lacks sufficient depth, when hiding an unattractive frame, or when making a window appear larger than it is.

P

Plantation Shutters

Plantation shutters are interior window coverings featuring wide louvers, typically 2.5 to 4.5 inches, mounted in a solid frame. They tilt open and closed to control light and privacy while adding a permanent, architectural look to a home. They are often considered a value-adding upgrade for resale.

R

Roller Shades

Roller shades are a single panel of fabric that rolls up onto a tube at the top of the window. They are available in light-filtering, room-darkening, and blackout opacities, and can be motorized for hands-free operation.

Roman Shades

Roman shades are fabric window coverings that fold into smooth, horizontal pleats when raised and lie flat when lowered. They combine the soft look of a drapery with the function of a shade, available in flat-fold, hobbled, or relaxed styles.

Room Darkening

Room-darkening treatments block most incoming light but may still allow some bleed around the edges of the window. They are a step below blackout and are popular for living rooms, nurseries, and bedrooms where complete darkness is not required.

S

Smart Shades

Smart shades are motorized window treatments that integrate with home automation platforms like Alexa, Google Home, or Apple HomeKit. They can be programmed on schedules, triggered by sunlight sensors, or controlled by voice for convenience and energy savings.

Solar Shades

Solar shades are roller-style treatments made from a woven mesh fabric that blocks UV rays and reduces heat gain while preserving outside views. Openness factor, typically 1% to 14%, determines how much light and visibility passes through the fabric.

T

Top-Down Bottom-Up

Top-down bottom-up is a shade operating system that allows the covering to be lowered from the top or raised from the bottom. This flexibility lets natural light enter from above while maintaining privacy at eye level, popular for street-facing windows.

V

Vertical Blinds

Vertical blinds use long, vertical slats or vanes that rotate to control light and slide open along a track. They are commonly used on sliding glass doors, patio doors, and oversized windows where horizontal treatments are impractical.
