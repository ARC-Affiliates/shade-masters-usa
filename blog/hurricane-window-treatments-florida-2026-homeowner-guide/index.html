What Are Hurricane Window Treatments in Florida?

Hurricane window treatments are window coverings or barriers engineered to resist wind pressures and impact from flying debris during a tropical storm or hurricane.

In Florida, these products must carry a Florida Product Approval or Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance to satisfy building code in wind-borne debris regions.

The category includes impact-rated shutters (rigid exterior barriers tested to withstand a 9-pound 2x4 traveling at 34 mph), accordion shutters, roll-down shutters, Bahama and colonial shutters, and removable storm panels. Interior treatments like plantation shutters or honeycomb shades do not provide impact protection on their own, but they pair well with impact-rated glass or exterior shutters for daily light control. According to Shade Masters USA, most Leesburg homeowners combine an exterior storm system with interior shading for a complete solution.

Which Hurricane Window Treatment Options Work Best in Leesburg?

Hurricane window treatment selection is the process of matching a storm-rated product to your home's architecture, budget, and HOA rules.

For most Leesburg homes, accordion or roll-down shutters offer the best balance of protection, convenience, and resale value.

  • Accordion shutters — permanently mounted, deploy in minutes, mid-range cost.
  • Roll-down shutters — concealed in a headbox, motorized options available, premium price.
  • Bahama shutters — top-hinged, double as shade awnings, common in lakefront homes near Lake Griffin.
  • Colonial shutters — side-hinged decorative panels with storm bars, popular in historic downtown Leesburg.
  • Storm panels — lowest cost, stored off-window, require manual installation before each storm.
  • Impact-rated glass with interior plantation shutters or honeycomb shades — clean look, no deployment needed, highest upfront cost.

Roll-down vs accordion: Roll-down shutters are the convenience leader because motorized units deploy at the press of a button. Accordion shutters are the value leader because they cost 30% to 40% less while still meeting code.

What Does Florida Building Code Require in 2026?

The Florida Building Code is the statewide construction standard that sets wind-load and impact-protection rules for openings in hurricane-prone areas.

Lake County falls within the 130 mph wind zone, and any window protection product must carry Florida Product Approval or Miami-Dade NOA documentation.

Per Florida Building Code, Eighth Edition (2023) Section 1609.2, all exterior glazed openings in wind-borne debris regions must be protected by an impact-resistant covering or impact-rated glass (source: floridabuilding.org). Lake County requires a building permit for shutter installation under Lake County Code Chapter 6. As of 2026, permit fees in unincorporated Lake County run $75 to $200 per single-family installation.

How Do You Verify the Right Installer?

Verification is the process of confirming an installer's license, insurance, product approvals, and reputation before signing a contract.

Run a five-minute background check on the DBPR website and request product approval numbers in writing before any deposit.

  1. Confirm Florida contractor license status at myfloridalicense.com.
  2. Request a current Certificate of Insurance naming you as additional insured.
  3. Ask for Florida Product Approval or Miami-Dade NOA numbers for each product.
  4. Verify the installer pulls the building permit (never sign a homeowner-pulled permit).
  5. Read at least 20 recent Google reviews dated within the last 12 months.
  6. Get three written quotes for direct comparison.
  7. Confirm written warranty terms — minimum 5 years on hardware, 10 on motors.

#Red flags to watch for

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Taping windows with masking tape protects against hurricane debris.

Fact: Tape provides zero impact protection and is discouraged by the National Hurricane Center.

Myth: Plantation shutters alone qualify as hurricane protection.

Fact: Interior plantation shutters are not impact-rated. They must pair with impact glass or exterior storm shutters.

Myth: Storm protection is only for coastal homes.

Fact: Lake County is inside the 130 mph wind-borne debris region per Florida Building Code.

Myth: Insurance discounts are not worth the install cost.

Fact: Wind mitigation credits in Florida average 10% to 45% off the wind portion of the premium per state filings.
